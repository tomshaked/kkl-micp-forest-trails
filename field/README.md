# Field Test — Phase 3 (Months 16–24)

This phase applies and monitors MICP stabilization on a real KKL-JNF forest trail segment, comparing it to an untreated control on the same trail.

---

## Objectives

- Demonstrate real-world feasibility of robotic MICP application on a forest trail
- Quantify long-term performance across key durability and maintenance metrics
- Generate comparative data to support adoption decisions by KKL-JNF

---

## Site Selection Criteria

- KKL-JNF-managed trail with accessible ~10 m test segment
- Adjacent ~10 m control segment on the same trail (same soil type, slope, and traffic)
- Representative of a common Israeli forest soil and climate zone
- Accessible for robotic equipment deployment

---

## Application

The test segment will be treated using the robotic protocols developed in Phase 2:
- Robotic MICP spraying for surface stabilization
- Optional 3D-printed surface elements where design testing is relevant

The control segment receives no treatment and continues standard maintenance.

---

## Monitoring Protocol

Measurements taken at defined time points over 12 months (post-application):

| Metric | Method | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Surface strength | Clegg Hammer (Mohajerani et al., 2020) | Monthly |
| Crack area | Photographic survey + image analysis | Monthly |
| Surface erosion / displacement | Topographic scan or pin erosion measurement | Quarterly |
| Vegetation regrowth | Visual survey + % cover estimate | Monthly |
| Maintenance events | Log of interventions required | Ongoing |

Both test and control segments are measured under the same conditions at each time point.

---

## Success Criteria

- Higher surface strength (Clegg value) in treated vs. control segment
- Reduced crack formation and erosion over the monitoring period
- Lower vegetation regrowth rate on treated surface
- Fewer or no maintenance interventions required on treated segment

---

## References

- Mohajerani, A., et al. (2020). Clegg impact hammer: An equipment for evaluation of the strength characteristics of pavement materials. *Road Materials and Pavement Design, 21*(2), 467–485.
