# Laboratory Experiments — Phase 1 (Months 1–9)

This phase develops and validates MICP-treated soil mixtures before robotic application and field deployment.

---

## Objectives

- Identify optimal MICP treatment parameters for forest soil types found in KKL-JNF sites
- Quantify improvements in mechanical strength, cracking resistance, water permeability, and vegetation suppression
- Produce data to inform robotic application parameters in Phase 2

---

## Tasks

### 1.1 Soil-MICP Mixture Development
- Collect soil samples from representative KKL-JNF forest sites
- Apply biostimulation with urea, calcium chloride (CaCl₂), and yeast extract to activate indigenous ureolytic bacteria
- Monitor pH and urea concentration over treatment cycles as indicators of biochemical activity
- Quantify calcite precipitation (CaCO₃ content by mass)

### 1.2 Mechanical and Physical Testing
Testing follows standard ASTM methods:

| Test | Standard | Metric |
|------|----------|--------|
| Unconfined Compressive Strength (UCS) | ASTM D2166 | Soil bearing capacity |
| Hydraulic Conductivity | ASTM D5084 | Water permeability |
| Cracking resistance | Visual + image analysis | Crack area fraction |
| Vegetation growth | Controlled germination trials | Germination rate on treated surface |

All treated specimens are compared to untreated control samples from the same soil batch.

---

## Success Criteria

- Measurable increase in UCS relative to untreated controls
- Reduction in hydraulic conductivity
- Reduced crack formation over drying cycles
- Reduced germination rate on treated surfaces
